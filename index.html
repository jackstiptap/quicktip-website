How It Works
Three taps. That's it.
No cash, no awkward moments, no hunting for an ATM.
1

Open the app

Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) automatically discovers service workers nearby. No scanning QR codes, no searching usernames — they just appear. Like magic.

2

Pick your amount

Choose a preset or slide to a custom amount. Pay with Apple Pay, Google Pay, a saved card, or directly from your bank account.

3

Tip sent instantly

The worker gets a push notification immediately. Funds hit their bank account on a regular payout schedule.

FAQ
Questions? We've got answers.

Yes — completely free for customers. Workers pay a small platform fee on tips received, designed to keep costs as low as possible for workers. Standard payment processing applies.

Workers broadcast a Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) signal from the worker app. When a customer opens their app nearby, available workers appear automatically — no pairing, no QR codes, no typing names. Workers don't even need their phone out — just toggle on visibility from the dashboard and put it in their pocket.

Absolutely. All payments are processed by Stripe, the same platform trusted by Amazon, Shopify, and millions of businesses. Your card details are never stored on our servers — they go directly to Stripe's PCI-compliant infrastructure.

Workers connect a bank account through Stripe during setup. Tips accumulate in their account balance and are paid out on a regular schedule. Payouts typically arrive in 1–2 business days.

Anyone who receives tips as part of their service — valets, bellhops, doormen, nail technicians, barbers, caddies, dockhands, coat check attendants, housekeeping, shuttle drivers, and more. If you get tipped, this app is for you.

Customers need Bluetooth enabled to discover workers. Workers also need Bluetooth on, but don't need to have their phone out — just toggle on visibility from the dashboard and put the phone in your pocket. Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) has minimal battery impact. Workers can also generate a QR code as a backup option.

Customers can pay with Apple Pay, Google Pay, any saved credit or debit card, or directly from their bank account. Paying with bank is recommended because it has lower processing fees — meaning workers keep more of each tip.
